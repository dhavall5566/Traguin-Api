DAY 02
KOHIMA SIGHTSEEING - WAR MEMORIAL, MUSEUMS & LOCAL BRAIDED
MARKETS
Savor an exquisite locally sourced organic breakfast before diving into the core historical elements of the
town. Your day begins with a highly emotional, curated walk through the globally recognized Kohima War
Cemetery, a beautifully manicured memorial dedicated to the brave soldiers who halted the Japanese
advance in World War II. Walk past the famous historic tennis court grounds and read the moving epitaphs
that encapsulate global history. 
Next, dive deep into the indigenous roots of the hills with an exclusive tour of the State Museum, housing rare
tribal dynamic relics, authentic traditional weapons, and majestic structural models of different Naga tribes.
Conclude your daytime sightseeing at the bustling local markets where the indigenous community trades local
bamboo products, fresh organic herbs, handwoven fabrics, and intricate tribal jewelry. 
Sightseeing Included: WWII Kohima War Cemetery, Nagaland State Museum, Kohima Cathedral (largest
church structure in Asia), and the vibrant Local Bazaar. 
Optional Activities: A private traditional loin-loom weaving demonstration arranged exclusively for your
family.

DAY 03
THE CULTURAL CRADLE OF KISAMA HERITAGE VILLAGE
Today promises an immersive historical fairytale as we transfer you to Kisama Heritage Village, the official
permanent venue of India’s world-famous Hornbill Festival. Built explicitly like a living museum, Kisama
showcases grand, architecturally stunning 'Morungs' (traditional communal youth dormitories) belonging to all
17 distinct major tribes of Nagaland. 
Your family can marvel at the magnificent woodcarvings, massive log-drums, and traditional tribal architecture
up close without the bustling festival rush, allowing for a deep, tranquil, educational family experience. Your
expert guide will narrate the intense folklore behind the warrior tattoos, ceremonial hornbill feathers, and
majestic spears. This is the top popular Instagram location in the region, perfect for memorable family
portraits in traditional backdrops. 
Sightseeing Included: Full exploration of Kisama Morungs, World War II Museum complex, and the scenic
slopes of Kigwema traditional village. 
Optional Activities: Renting authentic tribal ceremonial shawls and headgears for an exclusive professional
family photoshoot. 
Evening Experience: A private bonfire and storytelling session at the village outskirts. 
Overnight Stay: Kohima. 
Meals Included: Breakfast & Authentic Naga-Style Dinner. 
DAY 04
KHONOMA VILLAGE - THE FIRST GREEN VILLAGE OF ASIA
Prepare for an awe-inspiring day as we head to the globally celebrated Khonoma Green Village, an ancient
700-year-old settlement known globally for its pioneering ecological conservation, absolute ban on hunting,
and breathtaking stepped terraced agriculture. Nestled amidst pristine, mist-shrouded green mountains,
Khonoma tells a tale of incredible defiance against British colonial forces and a subsequent transition to
absolute global conservation.

IMPORTANT TRAVEL NOTES & POLICIES
Inner Line Permits: It is mandatory for Indian domestic citizens to possess valid Inner Line Permits (ILP)
to enter Nagaland. TRAGUIN will facilitate all documentation seamlessly prior to your arrival.
Weather & Clothing: Kohima enjoys cool, crisp mountain weather. Lightweight jackets are necessary for
daytime, while thick woolens are highly recommended for chilly nights.
Local Customs: Naga communities are deeply rooted in heritage and local values. Guests are kindly
requested to seek permission before clicking close-up portraits of village elders.
Advance Booking Notice: Due to limited luxury inventory in the Northeast circuit, bookings should ideally
be confirmed at least 45–60 days prior to travel.
